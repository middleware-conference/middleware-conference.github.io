City of Davis

Middleware 2019 welcomes you to the small, yet beautiful town of Davis. Davis is located just 12 miles away from the capital of the state of California, Sacramento. Davis is considered as one of the safest cities in USA and has a diverse and welcoming community that houses people of all color.


University of California Davis

The city of Davis is a home for Aggies, a term used for students of University of California Davis.

  • Founded in 1905, UC Davis boasts a campus of 5,300 acres and is a pilgrimage for 36,000 students.
  • Ranked 6th Amongst Public Universities.
  • Ranked 8thin America’s Coolest Schools.
  • Ranked 10th Happiest U.S. College Campus.
  • Ranked Number One Diverse University in USA.
  • Most respected college for winemaking and grape-growing in America.
  • Among the top two universities in the world for Agriculture and Winemaking.

Why Davis?

  • One of the most educated city in USA.
  • Home to one of the largest farm-to-table, farm-to-fork social movement that cherishes local and organic produce at affordable prices.
  • The new home of United States Bicycling Hall of Fame (bike is the dominant form of transportation all year round).
  • A highly affordable town with a significantly cheaper conference organizing facilities and accommodation compared to the Bay Area.
  • Over 50 different restaurants catering the taste buds of Asian, European, Latin and Mediterranean Middleware community.
  • Several Wine and Craft Beer Bars in Downtown Davis.
    • Davis Beer Shoppe has one of the largest selection of local and international beers.
    • VINI Wine Bar has a state-of-the-art Enomatic wine serving system that allows people to choose and taste any of the rotating selection of 72 wines.

What is near Davis?

  • Proximity to Silicon Valley, a great opportunity to bring together practitioners and academics in order to facilitate industry impacts while gaining industry sponsorship and support (two hours away).
  • Just half an hour away from The California State Capitol, home to the government of California and serves both as a museum and the state’s working seat of government.
  • An hour drive to Napa Valley, which is a home to more than 400 wineries.
  • An hour and half drive to San Francisco, the tech capital of the world!
  • An hour and half drive to the divine and pristine Lake Tahoe, voted as the America's best Lake.
  • A three hour drive to the majestic Yosemite National Park, known for its dramatic waterfalls, giant Sequoias, abundant wildlife, and awe-inspiring cliffs, such as Half Dome and El Capitan.
